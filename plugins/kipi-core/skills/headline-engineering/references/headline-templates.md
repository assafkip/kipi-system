# Headline Templates by Platform

Proven shapes with char counts and examples. Use as the menu when drafting headlines.

## X (Twitter) hook templates

Target: under 110 chars total. Sweet spot: 71-100. First 5-7 words must hold the punch.

### Template X1 — The absurd unit reveal (Pattern A)
Format: `[surprisingly small unit] [verb] [much bigger result]`
Examples:
- "1 year of prompts. A 21-byte file fixed it." (44 chars)
- "$3M in ads. A 100-word email beat them all." (44 chars)
- "47 frameworks tested. The smallest one won." (44 chars)

When to use: when the actual artifact size or count is unexpectedly small relative to the impact.

### Template X2 — The "I'm done with X" confession (Pattern B)
Format: `I'm done with [common practice].`
Examples:
- "I'm done with using local LLMs for coding." (41 chars, 1K upvotes)
- "I cannot go back to claude now" (30 chars, 1.2K upvotes)

When to use: when reversing a public stance or default approach.

### Template X3 — The "X admits to Y" exposé (Pattern C)
Format: `[Company] admits to [unflattering thing].`
Examples:
- "Anthropic admits to have made hosted models more stupid" (54 chars, 1.3K upvotes)
- "PSA: Claude Pro no longer lists Claude Code as an included feature" (66 chars, 3K upvotes)

When to use: when surfacing something the company would rather not lead with.

### Template X4 — The specific feat (Pattern D)
Format: `[Specific number with units] [verb] [specific thing]`
Examples:
- "ChatGPT 5.4 Solved a 64-Year-Old Math Problem" (45 chars, 13K upvotes)
- "I gained 12,847 followers in 63 days" (37 chars)

When to use: when the actual number is unusually specific and the achievement is concrete.

## Reddit headline templates

Target: 30-66 chars cluster. Subreddit norms matter — r/LocalLLaMA tolerates technical specificity; r/ChatGPT rewards emotional shapes.

### Template R1 — The confession with timeline
Format: `After [time period] of [practice], here's what actually works`
Examples:
- "After 6 months of daily AI pair programming, here's what actually works (and what's just hype)" (cross-posted in 3+ subs)
- "11 Claude things I wish someone had told me 12 months ago" (high engagement)

### Template R2 — The naive question (Pattern E)
Format: `Forgive my ignorance but [counter-intuitive question]?`
Examples:
- "Forgive my ignorance but how is a 27B model better than 397B?" (1,167 upvotes)
- "How is X better than Y?"

### Template R3 — The "I asked X to..." setup (Pattern F)
Format: `I asked [AI] to [unusual prompt]`
Examples:
- "I asked ChatGPT to imagine itself in retirement" (17,152 upvotes)
- "I asked ChatGPT for a 'Perfectly Normal Family Picnic', but told it to hide a few subtle details"

### Template R4 — The PSA
Format: `PSA: [breaking observation about a product/policy]`
Examples:
- "PSA: Claude Pro no longer lists Claude Code as an included feature" (2,978 upvotes)

## LinkedIn long-form templates

Target: 60-100 chars. Personal story arc baked in.

### Template L1 — The years-of-failure setup
Format: `[Time period] of [common practice]. [Counter-intuitive fix].`
Examples:
- "I spent a year on AI memory. A folder fixed it."
- "5 years of GTM. A single Notion table changed everything."

### Template L2 — The witness moment
Format: `At [named company], I watched [specific failure pattern].`
Examples:
- "At Google, I watched four teams fight the same scam operation."
- "At Meta, I watched threat intel ship to nowhere."

### Template L3 — The named-dysfunction title
Format: `The [name] problem nobody is naming.`
Examples:
- "The signal-sharing mirage."
- "The whac-a-mole metric."

## Medium / Substack titles

Target: 50-90 chars. Allows subtitles.

### Template M1 — The declarative concrete object
Format: `The [specific object] that [outcome]`
Examples:
- "The 21-byte file that runs my consulting practice." (51 chars, original article 1 title)
- "The eight-line script that replaced my CRM."

### Template M2 — The contrarian "Why X" (only with a specific number)
Format: `Why [specific number] of [thing] [specific outcome]`
Examples:
- "Why 73% of enterprise AI rollouts fail before they start"
Avoid generic "Why X Matters" — those don't land.

### Template M3 — The compound-failure title
Format: `[Compound math claim or counter-stat]`
Examples:
- "85% reliable per step. 20% reliable per workflow."

## Banned title patterns (use NONE of these)

These shapes do not appear in any top-performing AI content in 2026:

- "The Ultimate Guide to X"
- "X Things You Need to Know About Y"
- "Why X Matters" (without a number or scar)
- "How to X" (too soft on every platform)
- "Mastering X"
- "Unlocking the Power of Y"
- "X: A Comprehensive [Guide / Look / Analysis]"
- "In Today's Fast-Paced World..."
- "Top X" / "Best Y" / "Essential Z" listicle promises (unless paired with an unusually specific number — "47 things I tested" beats "10 things")
- Em dash subtitles (no em dashes period)

## The first-7-words audit

Before shipping any title, cover everything after word 7. Does the hook still earn the click?

Pass:
- "1 year of prompts. A 21-byte file fixed it." → cover after word 7 = "1 year of prompts. A 21-byte file" — still hooks
- "ChatGPT 5.4 Solved a 64-Year-Old Math Problem" → "ChatGPT 5.4 Solved a 64-Year-Old Math" — still hooks

Fail:
- "I have been thinking a lot about how AI memory works lately" → cover after word 7 = "I have been thinking a lot about" — nothing
- "Why your AI workflow might benefit from some structural changes" → "Why your AI workflow might benefit from" — nothing

If word 8+ is doing the work, rewrite.

## Quality check (run before output)

1. Char count fits platform range
2. Punch is in first 5-7 words
3. At least one specific number, name, or concrete object
4. Identifiable pattern from A-F
5. No banned shapes from the banned list above
6. No em dashes
7. No hedge words ("might", "could", "perhaps")
8. No "Excited", "Thrilled", "Humbled" (gratitude tells)

Output only after all 8 pass.
