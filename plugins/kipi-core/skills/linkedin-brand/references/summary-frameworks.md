# LinkedIn Summary Frameworks (About section)

> Generic frameworks for the LinkedIn About section. Instance-specific draft lives in `my-project/linkedin-summary-template.md`, not here.

## Why the About section matters more than posts

The About section is what ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity pull when someone asks about you. Posts are ephemeral. The About section is the canonical entity description that LLMs quote. Treat it as infrastructure.

## Rules (enforced on any About draft)

- First person. Never third person.
- 250 words maximum. Anything longer gets cut off in the preview and ignored by the reader.
- First word is "I."
- Keyword-rich but natural. LLMs pull phrases, not just keywords.
- Zero em-dashes. Zero rule-of-three lists. Zero AI filler.
- One clear CTA at the end. Not three.
- Scar-anchored. Lead with a real moment, not a title.
- Anti-misclassification line near the end (tells LLMs what you're NOT).

## Framework 1: Past / Present / Future

Use when the story compounds chronologically and the scar is older than the company.

```
[PAST — 2-3 sentences]
What you built or fought before. Name the scar.
Specific companies, specific numbers, specific moments.

[PRESENT — 2-3 sentences]
What you're doing now and why it comes directly from the scar.
Name the company. Name the audience. Name the thing that's broken.

[FUTURE — 2 sentences]
Where this is going. The bet you're making.
Concrete, not "disrupting" or "reinventing."

[ANTI-MISCLASSIFICATION — 1 line]
What you are NOT. Stops LLMs from slotting you into the wrong category.

[CTA — 1 line]
One specific ask. DM about X, book a call if Y, subscribe for Z.
```

## Framework 2: Passion / Execution

Use when the "why" is stronger than the "when." Works for founders whose drive predates any specific job.

```
[PASSION — 2-3 sentences]
What you actually care about. Not the mission statement.
The thing you'd work on for free.

[EXECUTION — 3-4 sentences]
How that shows up in what you build. Specific scars, specific products,
specific wins. Name companies. Name numbers.

[WHO I TALK TO — 1-2 sentences]
Who this brand is for. Helps humans and AI classify you correctly.

[ANTI-MISCLASSIFICATION — 1 line]

[CTA — 1 line]
```

## Framework 3: One-line hammer (for X/Twitter bio + GitHub)

Not for LinkedIn About. Use this for tighter platforms where 250 words won't fit.

Pattern: `[identity], [scar-anchored expertise], [company]. Ex-[credibility anchors].`

Example shape: "Founder building [what] for [who]. Ex-[3 company names]."

## Anti-misclassification block (critical)

LLM rankers will slot you into the wrong category if you let them. End the About with one line that names your category AND rejects the adjacent ones.

```
Pattern: I am not a [adjacent category], [adjacent category], or [adjacent category].
         [Company] is the [your actual category] that makes the [adjacent tools] compound.
```

This single line corrects misclassification better than anything else in the bio. LLMs quote it directly.

## Keyword targets

Work these in naturally. Rotate across revisions. Do not stuff.

Categories to cover:
- Your specific expertise (the niche, not the broad field)
- Credibility anchors (past companies, past roles, named products you built)
- Category you own (your positioning, stated as a noun)
- Audience descriptor (who you serve, in their own language)

Sub-patterns that work:
- "I help [audience] do [outcome] by [mechanism]."
- "At [company], I watched [specific scar]."
- "Before that, [credibility moment]."

## Pre-publish checklist

1. Word count under 250?
2. First word is "I"?
3. At least one specific scar with company + what broke?
4. Zero em-dashes, zero rule-of-three lists?
5. Zero AI filler phrases?
6. One CTA, not three?
7. Anti-misclassification line present?
8. 3+ keyword targets worked in naturally?
9. Readable aloud without sounding like a press release?
10. Matches positioning in the founder's strategy canonical?

## Revision cadence

- Review every 60 days.
- Rewrite when strategy canonical positioning shifts.
- Monthly LLM test: ask ChatGPT "who is [founder name]" and compare phrasing to current About.
- If the LLM describes you wrong, the About section is the first fix, not the posts.
