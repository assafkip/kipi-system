#### kipi-investigations — Compounding intel memory <!-- pin -->
- **Audience:** Boutique intel firm / small T&S team operator (e.g., Ally)
- **Context:** When a customer describes "I have N reports and can't find connections across them"
- **The line:** "You drop your reports in. It builds the knowledge graph. Next report you drop in, it tells you which actor you've seen before and where."
- **Why it works:** Mirrors the pain back without jargon. Frames compounding memory as a verb, not a feature.

#### kipi-investigations — BYO data, no integration
- **Audience:** Small T&S team operator who has been burned by enterprise vendor integration cycles
- **Context:** Differentiating from Palantir/Babel/Maltego
- **The line:** "It doesn't pull from your data systems. You bring your intel reports. It builds the layer on top."
- **Why it works:** Removes the "we need IT involvement" objection. Resource-constrained teams can deploy without procurement.
