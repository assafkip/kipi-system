# Mom Test Playbook -- Customer Discovery Questions

{{SETUP_NEEDED}}

Based on "The Mom Test" by Rob Fitzpatrick. Questions that extract real data, not compliments.

## Core Rules
1. Talk about THEIR life, not your idea
2. Ask about specifics in the past, not generics about the future
3. Talk less, listen more

## Discovery Questions (by stage)

### Problem Discovery
- "What does your typical week look like?" (opens the door without leading)
- "What's the most annoying part of [process]?" (specific, not hypothetical)
- "Walk me through the last time [pain event] happened" (concrete, past tense)
- "How are you dealing with that today?" (reveals current solution and switching cost)
- "What have you tried?" (shows urgency and budget -- if they've tried nothing, pain isn't real)

### Solution Validation
- "If you had a magic wand, what would it do?" (reveals the real want)
- "What would have to be true for you to switch from [current solution]?" (switching cost)
- "Who else deals with this at your company?" (multi-stakeholder = bigger deal)

### Budget & Commitment
- "How much is this costing you today?" (quantified pain = budget exists)
- "Have you allocated budget to fix this?" (direct but necessary)
- "What would you pay to make this problem go away?" (anchoring)
- "If we built this, would you be our first customer?" (commitment test)

### Signals That Matter
| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| They pull out their phone to show you | Real pain, high engagement |
| They introduce you to someone | Commitment signal |
| They ask about pricing | Buying signal |
| They say "that would be nice" | Polite deflection, not a real signal |
| They give you a time commitment | Partial commitment |
