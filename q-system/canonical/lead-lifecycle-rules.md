# Lead Lifecycle Rules

> When to advance, park, re-engage, or kill a lead.

## Statuses

| Status | Meaning | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Cold | No relationship | Research, then warm up |
| Warm | Some engagement, no conversation | Continue engaging |
| Active | In conversation | Follow up, advance |
| Partner / Customer | Committed | Nurture, deliver |
| Cooling | Was active, going quiet | Recovery action or auto-close |
| Passed | Dead, closed | Archive |

## Auto-Close Rules

- **3 touches + no response + 14 days = Passed** (RULE-002)
- No founder decision needed. System closes and reports the count.
- Re-engagement triggers can revive Passed contacts (see below)

## Re-Engagement Triggers

These events allow re-engaging a Passed or Cooling contact:
- **In-person encounter** (conference, event, meetup)
- **They post about a relevant topic** (detected in daily scrape)
- **Mutual connection offers intro**
- **They change roles/companies** (detected via LinkedIn)
- **You ship something they specifically asked about**

## Parking Rules

- Contact showed interest but timing is wrong = Park with a future date
- Parked contacts auto-surface on their date in the morning briefing
- No action required until the date arrives
